# Knowledge Base

The Knowledge Base contains practical guides for getting the best results with your Bambu Lab printer. Here you will find concrete numbers and tips based on experience with the P1S, X1C, and A1.

## What will you find here?

### Filaments — standard materials
Detailed guides for each material type — temperature settings, plate selection, drying times, and tips for successful printing.

- [PLA](./filaments/pla) — The easiest to print, recommended for beginners
- [PETG](./filaments/petg) — Stronger than PLA, good chemical resistance
- [ABS](./filaments/abs) — Thermally stable, requires enclosure
- [PC (Polycarbonate)](./filaments/pc) — Extremely impact-resistant and heat-stable
- [TPU](./filaments/tpu) — Flexible material, requires low speed
- [PA / Nylon](./filaments/nylon) — Extremely wear-resistant, demanding to print

### Filaments — composites, specials, and references

- [Composite materials (CF/GF)](./filaments/composite) — PLA-CF, PETG-CF, PA-CF, PA-GF, ABS-CF etc. — hardened steel nozzle required
- [Special materials](./filaments/special) — ASA (UV-resistant), PC, PP, PVA, HIPS and other niche materials
- [Bambu Lab series](./filaments/bambu-series) — PLA Basic/Matte/Silk/Sparkle/Tough/Glow/Aero/Carbon, PETG Basic/HF/CF, ABS, ASA, TPU, PA6-CF/GF, PC, PVA, HIPS — all with RFID and AMS compatibility
- [Master temperature guide](./filaments/guide) — One lookup with nozzle/bed/chamber for 15+ materials
- [Material comparison](./filaments/comparison) — Strength, heat, flex, UV, chemical resistance + Shore hardness + HDT/VST
- [Compatibility matrix](./filaments/compatibility) — Which materials fit which build plates and nozzles
- [Print profiles and settings](./filaments/profiles) — Retract, speed, cooling, and quality presets

The Knowledge Base is for you who:
- Are new to 3D printing with Bambu Lab

- Have a specific problem you want to solve
